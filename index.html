The Six Astrotourism Activities

01 / DOME EXPERIENCE

Digital Planetarium Shows

A portable, climate-controlled, inflatable dome theater that utilizes advanced digital systems to project high-fidelity astronomical visuals onto a hemispherical screen. It provides students and tourists with an immersive journey through deep space, rendering stellar clusters, nebulae, and space exploration shows in Hindi and English.

Department Integration Specs
  • Daily Capacity: 1,000+ Students
  • Footprint Needed: 30 x 30 ft (Flat Ground)
  • Power: 5kW Constant Source
02 / CELESTIAL OBSERVATION

Planet & Solar Observations

Safely observe our parent star in real-time through specialized refractors equipped with professional-grade Hydrogen-alpha and white-light solar filters during the day. After sunset, utilize computerized motorized GoTo tracking telescopes to resolve planetary rings, lunar craters, and deep-sky nebulae under dark skies.

Department Integration Specs
  • Hardware: 2x Motorized & 1x Manual Telescopes
  • Solar Filters: Daytime H-Alpha & Binoculars
  • Nocturnal Views: Saturn, Jupiter, Moon
03 / SIMULATION ZONE

VR Space Walk Experience

A high-fidelity virtual reality zone simulating space walks, orbital station tours, and rover missions. Using interactive 6-DOF headsets, visitors experience simulated zero-gravity orbits and lunar terrain traverses, bridging the gap between textbook theory and state-of-the-art public engagement.

Department Integration Specs
  • Equipment: 2x VR Headset Stations
  • Simulation: ISS Astronaut Spacewalk
  • Throughput: 20-30 users per hour
04 / ANCIENT TIMEKEEPING

Workshop on Sundials

A hands-on workshop where participants construct functioning equatorial sundials. By aligning their sundial with local coordinates, students calculate solar time and analyze Earth's axial tilt, directly linking India's historical archeo-astronomy with modern astrophysics.

Department Integration Specs
  • Session Format: DIY Classroom & Families
  • Takeaway: Functional equatorial sundial kits
  • Focus Area: Solar orientation & Earth's geometry
05 / INTERACTIVE KIOSKS

Gravity Kiosks

Measure and compare user weight across different celestial bodies using electronic planetary weight scales. This interactive kiosk setup calculates real-time gravitational differences on Mars, Jupiter, the Moon, and other planets, teaching gravitational physics in a fun, tactile way.

Department Integration Specs
  • Quantity: 4 Digital Gravity Stations
  • Display: Digital planetary indicators
  • User Output: Gravitational coefficient prints
06 / AEROSPACE ZONE

Pop Rocket Demonstration

A hands-on aerospace engineering challenge using DIY soda pop rocket kits. Participants explore Newton's third law of motion by mixing safe chemical propellants to build up gas pressure, culminating in high-altitude vertical launches that illustrate launch dynamics and trajectory angles.

Department Integration Specs
  • Kits Provided: DIY soda pop rocket packs
  • Science Goal: Chemical reaction & propulsion physics
  • Safety Level: 100% safe, non-toxic components
Sovereign Partnership Rationale

Why Sponsoring Astrotourism Drives State Development

01

Heritage Monument Night Activation

Historical forts, ancient sun temples, and heritage monuments are invaluable public assets. Sponsoring astrotourism campaigns allows tourism boards to safely activate these sites during post-sunset hours. Visitors engage in celestial observations against a historic architectural backdrop, extending visitor dwell time and creating local economic opportunities without requiring permanent structural modifications.

02

Sustainable Eco-Tourism (Dark Sky Preservation)

Astrotourism represents a sustainable, zero-impact infrastructure model. Sponsoring this initiative enables states to leverage natural dark skies in rural, forest, and desert regions as key assets. This attracts high-value, eco-conscious travelers, channeling revenue directly to homestays, local guides, and community operations without ecological disruption.

03

Youth STEM Empowerment & Skill Building

Aligning campaigns with regional educational programs brings high-quality scientific laboratories to rural schools. Local youth are trained in basic astronomy and telescope operations, equipping them to serve as local dark-sky tourism guides. This directly supports the constitutional goal of developing a scientific temper and creates new vocational pathways.

Flagship Campaign

Antariksh Darshan: Hamirpur

A landmark collaboration between the District Administration of Hamirpur and SPACE Group, establishing a dark-sky observatory at the Yamuna Bank to celebrate the district's annual festival.

1,000+
Students & Families Mobilized Daily
5 Days
Yamuna Bank Site Activation

In March 2026, the District Administration of Hamirpur, under the visionary leadership of District Magistrate Shri Ghanshyam Meena (IAS), partnered with SPACE Group to organise an exclusive astrotourism pavilion titled 'Antariksh Darshan'. The campaign served as the headline cultural and scientific anchor of the annual Hamirpur Mahotsav celebrations.

The chosen venue was the scenic Yamuna Bank in Hamirpur District, Uttar Pradesh. By setting up high-end computerized GoTo tracking telescopes and mobile planetarium domes, the campaign blended the natural beauty of the river banks with modern space science, offering local families and regional tourists an unprecedented night-sky experience.

Official Event Video: Antariksh Darshan Hamirpur Mahotsav Walkthrough

Beyond public stargazing, the campaign hosted structured workshops. Space tutors and astronomers guided visitors through planetary scales, solar filters, and pop rocket launches. This hands-on approach sparked a strong interest in STEM disciplines among the local youth, aligning perfectly with district-level educational and scientific outreach objectives.

Heritage Site Campaign

Antariksh Darshan: Mahoba

A strategic public-private partnership with the District Administration of Mahoba, activating the 9th-century Rahilya Sun Temple to blend architectural heritage with modern space science.

900+
Government School Students Sourced
10+
Rural School Clusters Participating

In March 2026, to mark the district's Foundation Day, the District Administration of Mahoba, in strategic collaboration with Space India, launched 'Antariksh Darshan Mahoba'. The initiative aimed to address two primary administrative objectives: revitalizing interest in local historical heritage and bringing high-quality science education to marginalized rural students in the Bundelkhand region.

The chosen venue was the historic 9th-century Rahilya Sun Temple, built by the Chandel rulers. This ancient stone structure, designed to track solar alignments, provided the perfect historical backdrop for a modern space-science campaign. The event successfully merged the architectural solar-timekeeping legacy of the past with the advanced telescopic observation of the present.

Official Event Video: Antariksh Darshan Mahoba Temple Stargazing

Selected government school students received special training in data analysis, participating directly in campaigns to discover near-Earth objects. The evening stargazing observation sessions opened to the general public, drawing local families, history enthusiasts, and travelers, creating a vibrant, family-friendly nocturnal community event around a previously unlit heritage site.

National Space Day

Antariksh Darshan: Jaipur

A historic public stargazing event at the Jantar Mantar observatory on National Space Day 2025, bridging traditional Indian astronomy with contemporary space achievements.

1,500+
Attendees & Students Mobilized
First Time
Public Observations since Independence

On August 23, 2025, to celebrate India's third National Space Day commemorating the historic landing of Chandrayaan-3 on the Moon, Rajasthan Tourism, in collaboration with SPACE India, hosted a landmark astronomical public outreach campaign at the UNESCO World Heritage Site Jantar Mantar in Jaipur.

This event marked the historic first-ever public astronomical observation at the Jantar Mantar observatory since independence. By blending the ancient stone instruments constructed by Maharaja Sawai Jai Singh II with modern motorized tracking telescopes and digital mobile planetariums, the campaign connected the rich legacy of historical Indian astronomy with today's state-of-the-art space achievements.

Glimpse of National Space Day Celebrations at Jantar Mantar, Jaipur by Rajasthan Tourism

Over 1,500 students, local families, and international tourists gathered at the venue to observe deep-sky objects and participate in engaging hands-on model rocketry workshops. The event showcased the immense potential of heritage sites to act as living science classrooms, boosting night tourism in Jaipur and fostering scientific curiosity across generations.
